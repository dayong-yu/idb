TARIFFS

WTO IDB and CTS Databases

The WTO has two main tariff databases - the Integrated Database (IDB) and the Consolidated Tariff Schedules (CTS).

  • Integrated Database (IDB) contains time series of official applied tariffs and matching imports of WTO members and acceding economies at the most detailed product level (usually referred to as the eight or more-digit product or tariff line code).
  • Consolidated Tariff Schedules (CTS) contains the commitments or the agreed maximum level of duty that can be imposed on imported products from other WTO Members.

IDB - History 

By a decision in November 1987 (BISD 34S/66), the GATT Council launched the Integrated Data Base (IDB) with a view to having official, updated, reliable and complete data needed to prepare reviews and assessments of tariffs concessions during market access negotiations. The Committee on Market Access (CMA) established on 31 January 1995 through a General Council decision (WT/L/47) oversees the content and operation of, and access to, the IDB.

The IDB is covered by several CMA decisions (G/MA/238, G/MA/239) and is referred to in the Transparency Mechanism on Preferential Trade Arrangements (PTA-TM) (WT/L/806) and in the Transparency Mechanism of Regional Trade Agreements (WT/L/671). The updated and latest CMA decision on the IDB adopted on 28 May 2019 (G/MA/367), which contains the modalities and operation of the IDB, consolidates the fragmented rules governing the IDB and takes into consideration the changes in technology to streamline and facilitate the preparation of notifications by Members.

IDB - Contents 

The IDB contains the official time series of applied tariffs and imports of WTO members and selected acceding countries. Members notify on an annual basis, data on:

  • Applied MFN import tariffs at the level of national customs tariff nomenclature (at the most detailed level, for example HS codes with 8, 9, or 10 digits, as normally applied by the Member's customs administration);

  • Import statistics in the same national tariff nomenclature as the corresponding MFN applied tariffs for the same year (i.e. same HS version and with the same level of disaggregation), including value (in USD or national currency) and volume (quantity and unit), by country of origin and by tariff line;

  • For concerned members, data elements required by the Transparency Mechanism for Preferential Trade Arrangements (PTA-TM), are the non-reciprocal preferential applied tariffs and import statistics, including the list of countries or separate customs territories on which they apply. More information on the PTA-TM data elements included in the IDB are contained in paragraph 1 of G/MA/367.

  • Optional data which could include the following:

    • Preferential tariffs applied in the context of regional trade agreements (for example free trade agreements or customs unions) including the list of partners and the preferential imports under these agreements.

    • Other applied non-MFN tariffs, for example tariffs applied on imports originating in non WTO Members including the list of partners and the imports under the non-MFN trading regime.

    • Ad valorem equivalents (AVEs) of non-ad valorem (NAV) duties, as calculated by the Member.

    • Applied internal taxes and other duties and charges (ODCs) at the tariff line level.

    • Imports or proportion of imports (value and volume) under tariff rate quotas (TRQs).

IDB - Data Sources 

Data on the IDB are generally sourced from official notifications by member governments. However, the CMA decision adopted on 13 July 2009 (G/MA/239) entitled "Framework to Enhance IDB Notification Compliance" gave the Secretariat more flexibility to collect data from official national, regional or international sources under certain circumstances to increase the IDB coverage and to lessen the reporting burden of members. These additional sources are approved by the concerned member beforehand and data obtained from these so-called "framework sources" are implicitly approved by concerned member before these are disseminated as member’s own official data.

Notification Deadlines
Members shall notify no later than :

  • 30 March, MFN and other applied tariffs of the current year,
  • 31 October, the import statistics of the previous calendar year

Dissemination Portals for IDB and CTS 

The contents of the IDB and CTS, as well as statistical indicators calculated from the databases are disseminated in an annual tariff publication and several online portals.

  • World Tariff Profiles is a joint publication prepared by the WTO Secretariat, the International Trade Centre (ITC) and UNCTAD. It contains comprehensive summary information on the tariffs and non-tariff measures imposed by over 170 countries and customs territories.

  • The Tariff Analysis Online (TAO) is an advanced online tools that allows users to extract detailed information for specific products, including bound and applied tariffs at the national tariff line level (HS-8 digits or more), matching import data by partner and other taxes related to importation, if notified. TAO has analytical reports and allows filtering criteria for analyses. Selected IDB data are restricted to authorized users only.

  • Tariff Download Facility (TDF) allows for mass data download of applied/bound tariffs and import statistics at the standard HS levels (2-,4-,6-digit).

  • Preferential Trade Arrangements (PTA) Database contains reference information on notified PTAs, serves as document repository, includes summary statistics of preferential tariffs and imports, including preference utilization.

  • WTO Data Portal (WTODATA) contains general statistical indicators of international trade, including bound and MFN applied tariffs.

  • Data maps is an interactive webpage that allows users to access visualization of different data elements, including trade and tariff data, and other international trade and market access data.

Dissemination Policy: The use and publication of IDB and CTS and related statistics are subject to certain conditions described in Annex 4 of document G/MA/367.

Jargon buster
  • ad valorem (AV): a tariff rate charged as percentage of the price, in contrast to non ad valorem duty which can be based on certain unit of measure (e.g., $5/kg).
  • applied rates: duties that are actually charged on imports. These can be below the bound rates.
  • bound rates (tariff binding): commitment not to increase a rate of duty beyond an agreed level. Once a rate of duty is bound, it may not be raised without compensating the affected parties
  • Harmonized System: World Customs Organization's international standard of coding used to identify products. More...
  • MFN (most-favoured-nation) tariff: normal non-discriminatory tariff charged on imports (excludes preferential tariffs under free trade agreements and other schemes or tariffs charged inside quotas) from other WTO members
  • schedules: (for goods) list of bound tariff rates
  • tariff line (TL): the code assigned to each product based on the HS. It should have at least six digits which is the most detailed standard HS code. Additional digits can be added by a trading economy to further classify its own products. National tariff lines usually have 8-digits.
